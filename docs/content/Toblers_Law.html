First Law of Geography

"Everything is related to everything else, but near things are more related than distant things."

-Waldo Tobler

First Law of Geography

This might seem obvious:

  • Students in the same class interact more.
  • Orca pods in different areas develop different dialects.
  • Hemlocks in Vancouver are more related to each other than to Hemlocks in New Brunswick.

First Law of Geography

There is nuance to the statement. It is not a grantee of similarity.

  • Vancouver's average snowfall is < 30 cm/yr
  • Grouse Mountain frequently exceeds 9 m/yr.

Spatial Heterogeneity

Uneven distribution across space, characteristic of many natural systems.

Map the Forest not the Trees

We don't need the location of every tree to map a forest. Use average presence of trees over a larger area instead!

Spatial Autocorrelation

Measure of similarity across space.

similarity across space

Natural systems are typically heterogeneous, but also spatially autocorrelated.

  • Allows a key assumptions when representing spatial data.
  • Closely linked to scale. What is heterogeneous at a large scale, could be homogeneous at a smaller scale.
